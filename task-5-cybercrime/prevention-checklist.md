# **Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students**



1. **Think before you click** **–** If a link or message looks suspicious, don’t click it. Scammers often make fake emails and messages look real.



**2. Use strong passwords** **–** Mix letters, numbers, and symbols. Don’t use the same password everywhere, and try to change them sometimes.



**3. Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA)** **–** Add extra protection for your email, social media, and UPI apps.



**4. Watch out for UPI scams –** Never approve payment requests from unknown numbers. Always double-check with the person before sending money.



**5. Keep apps updated –** Make sure your banking, UPI, and social media apps are always on the latest version. Updates fix security issues.



**6. Avoid public Wi-Fi for payments –** Don’t log in to banking or payment apps on public networks. Use mobile data or a secure connection.



**7. Don’t overshare personal info –** Be careful about what you post online. Scammers can use it to trick you.



**8. Backup important data –** Keep copies of important documents, photos, and files. This protects you in case of ransomware or data loss.



**9. Use privacy settings –** Adjust social media and app settings to control who sees your posts, info, and activity.



**10. Report suspicious activity –** If you face a scam, report it to the National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in⁠  or call 1930.
