# **Case Study: Phishing Attack**



Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers try to trick people into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or bank details. It usually happens through emails, messages, or fake websites that look genuine.



In a typical case, a person receives an email or message that appears to be from a trusted source like a bank or an online platform. The message often creates urgency, for example saying that the account will be blocked if action is not taken immediately. It includes a link that redirects the user to a fake website which looks almost identical to the real one. When the user enters their login details, the information is directly captured by the attacker.



People who are not very familiar with online security, including students and new internet users, are more likely to fall into this trap. Even working professionals can become victims if they are not careful.



The consequences of phishing can be serious. Victims may lose money, face unauthorized access to their accounts, or even suffer identity theft. In some cases, organizations also face data breaches and loss of trust.



This case study shows the importance of being careful while using digital platforms. Users should always check the authenticity of links, avoid sharing sensitive information, and use security measures like two-factor authentication to stay safe.
